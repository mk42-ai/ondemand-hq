The leader of Iran's negotiating team during peace talks with the US in Pakistan has said that the United States ultimately failed to gain the trust of the Iranian delegation. The American Vice President JD Vance said Iran did not accept Washington's terms. Pakistan stressed that it is imperative now that both sides observe the two-week ceasefire. Israel has continued to carry out air strikes on southern Lebanon. According to Lebanese state media, at least 11 people have been killed. Hezbollah also fired more rockets into northern Israel. People in Hungary are voting in record numbers in parliamentary elections, which could see the end of Prime Minister Viktor Orbán's 16 years in power. Most polls predict a victory for the center-right, pro-European Tisza Party of his main rival, Péter Magyar. One of India's best-known singers, Asha Bhosle, has died at the age of 92. She recorded songs for more than a thousand Bollywood films. The Guinness Book of World Records says she was the most recorded music artist anywhere. Ukraine and Russia have continued to accuse each other of violating a ceasefire to mark Orthodox Easter, although no major attacks were reported overnight. Both sides say the other side committed about 2,000 violations. The authorities in Haiti say at least 30 people have been killed in a stampede. The incident happened at an annual gathering at an historic fortress in the north of the country. The German football team Union Berlin have named Marie-Louise Eta as their new head coach, the first time a woman has been appointed to manage a men's side in one of Europe's top five leagues.
